## Why this exists

In 2024, cyber-insurance renewals were a 15-minute questionnaire. In 2026, they're a binder. Chubb, Travelers, and Beazley all now request "evidence of controls" alongside the questionnaire — and a missing piece can trigger a 30% premium increase or outright decline.

Most small businesses scramble for this evidence in the week before renewal. This binder, pre-formatted and filled out once per year, makes renewal a handoff instead of a scramble.

---

## The 9 tabs (full)

1. **Administrative Controls** — your written policies, who reviews them, how often
2. **MFA Evidence** — screenshots, export of MFA-enforcement settings from M365/Google Workspace, hardware-key inventory
3. **Backup Evidence** — backup schedule, retention, last test date, restore evidence
4. **Incident Response** — IR plan, tabletop exercise evidence, last incident debrief
5. **Training** — annual security training sign-in sheets, phishing simulation results
6. **Access Controls** — account provisioning/deprovisioning runbook, last quarterly audit
7. **Vendor Risk** — your vendor risk register (see our [$19 register](/store/vendor-risk-register) if you don't have one yet)
8. **Physical Security** — office-access logs, server-closet photos, badge system evidence
9. **Policy Documents** — WISP, HIPAA (if applicable), acceptable-use, remote-work, data-retention

## 30-day evidence-collection sprint (full)

Two weeks before renewal:
- Day 1: run access review → Tab 6 evidence
- Day 3: export MFA-enforcement reports → Tab 2
- Day 5: collect last three months of backup-test logs → Tab 3
- Day 7: update vendor risk register → Tab 7
- Day 10: gather training sign-in sheets + phishing simulation results → Tab 5
- Day 12: take fresh server-closet + badge-room photos → Tab 8
- Day 14: assemble binder, review with designated ISO, sign, scan a digital copy

The sprint template in the full kit breaks each of these down into an email-template-per-step so an office manager can execute without needing technical knowledge.

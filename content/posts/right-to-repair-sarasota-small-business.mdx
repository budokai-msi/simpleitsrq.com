This hit Hacker News this week: John Deere owners will soon have the right to repair their equipment, thanks to a settlement with the FTC. But what does this mean for small business owners in Sarasota, Bradenton, Venice, Lakewood Ranch, or Nokomis? In short, it's a win for anyone who relies on equipment to run their business. ## Short answer: The right to repair movement is all about giving owners and independent repair shops access to the information and parts they need to fix equipment, rather than being forced to go through the manufacturer. This can save time and money, and it's especially important for small businesses that can't afford downtime. ## Field note: As a managed IT services company, we've seen firsthand how frustrating it can be when equipment manufacturers limit access to repair information. It's not just about tractors - this issue affects all kinds of equipment, from medical devices to construction gear. ## What to do this week: Take stock of the equipment your business relies on, and research whether the manufacturer provides adequate repair information and support.

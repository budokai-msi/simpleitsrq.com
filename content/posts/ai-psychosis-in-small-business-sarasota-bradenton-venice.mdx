AI should make the team faster without making the business careless. The difference is a review loop: a clear rule for what the tool may draft and what a person must approve.

## Use AI where review is easy

Good first use cases have low stakes and obvious correction paths:

- Summarize meeting notes
- Draft a reply for a human to edit
- Categorize support tickets
- Pull action items from a call transcript
- Build a first checklist from an existing policy

That work saves time without quietly transferring judgment to a model.

## Keep humans on the line

Do not let AI approve refunds, change security settings, answer legal questions, diagnose patients, send payroll instructions, or commit the company to a promise. Those workflows need identity controls, audit trails, and written approval rules.

This hit Hacker News this week: the concept of AI psychosis, where companies become so reliant on artificial intelligence that they start to lose touch with reality. As a small business owner in Sarasota, Bradenton, Venice, Lakewood Ranch, or Nokomis, you might be wondering what this means for your organization. In simple terms, AI psychosis refers to the phenomenon where businesses start to prioritize automation over human judgment, leading to a decline in productivity and an increase in errors. This can happen when companies over-rely on AI tools to make decisions, without considering the potential consequences or limitations of these tools. For a small business, this can be particularly problematic, as it can lead to a loss of control and a decrease in the quality of work. ## Short answer: AI psychosis is a real risk for small businesses that adopt AI without careful consideration. As you explore AI solutions for your business, it's essential to strike a balance between automation and human oversight. ## Field note: I've seen this happen in local businesses, where the rush to adopt new technology leads to a lack of understanding of how it works and what its limitations are. This can lead to a range of problems, from data breaches to decreased customer satisfaction. ## What to do this week: Take a closer look at your AI adoption strategy and ask yourself if you're prioritizing automation over human judgment.

The risk is not that AI exists. The risk is that a team starts treating AI output as a decision instead of a draft. That is where bad customer messages, weak policies, and data leaks begin.

## What overreach looks like

You are probably moving too fast if:

- Staff paste client data into personal AI accounts
- Drafts go to customers without human review
- AI tools are purchased on individual credit cards
- Nobody can explain where prompts, uploads, or transcripts are stored
- The business cannot name who approves new AI tools

Those are operating problems, not innovation problems.

## A safer pattern

Use AI for drafts, summaries, classification, and first-pass research. Keep human approval on anything that changes money, contracts, health information, legal advice, customer commitments, or security settings.

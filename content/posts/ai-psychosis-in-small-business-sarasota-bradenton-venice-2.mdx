This hit Hacker News this week: the idea that some companies are suffering from 'AI psychosis', where their over-reliance on AI tools is actually hindering their ability to make good decisions. As a small business owner in Sarasota, Bradenton, Venice, Lakewood Ranch, or Nokomis, you might be wondering what this means for you. In short, it's a warning sign that you should be careful not to let AI tools dictate your every move. ## Short answer: AI psychosis occurs when a company becomes so reliant on AI that it loses sight of its core goals and values. This can lead to poor decision-making, decreased productivity, and a lack of creativity. ## Field note: I've seen this happen in small businesses where the owner is so enamored with the latest AI tool that they start to use it for every aspect of their business, without stopping to think about whether it's actually the best solution. For example, using AI to generate social media posts might seem like a great way to save time, but if the posts are low-quality or don't resonate with your audience, you're actually doing more harm than good. ## What to do this week: Take a step back and assess your use of AI tools in your business. Are you using them to augment your decision-making, or are you relying on them too heavily?

Old software stories are fun for engineers, but the small-office lesson is practical: systems last longer than anyone expects. The business has to know what it owns, how it is backed up, and who can still support it.

## The real risk

Legacy systems become dangerous when they are invisible:

- One workstation runs a program nobody else understands
- A vendor portal depends on an old browser or plugin
- Backups exist but were never restored
- License keys live in an email thread from years ago
- Nobody knows which device talks to the copier, camera system, or line-of-business app

That is how a small outage becomes a multi-day scramble.

## Document before replacing

Before ripping out old software, write down the workflow, files, credentials, dependencies, and recovery path. Then decide whether to keep, isolate, replace, or migrate it.

## Why your 2026 renewal looks different

If you renewed in 2024 or 2025, the questionnaire was maybe 20 questions. Your broker asked if you had antivirus, off-site backups, and multi-factor authentication on email. You said yes to all three (or something close to it), and rates moved 5-15%.

2026 is different. The three biggest small-business cyber carriers — Chubb, Travelers, and Beazley — each rewrote their renewal form in Q4 2025. The new forms run 40 to 60 questions. They ask for **evidence**, not just yes/no answers. They added an entire section on AI governance. And they added two questions whose wrong answer can trigger an outright decline, not just a rate increase.

Most Sarasota and Bradenton owners we talk to find this out on the quote call, then spend two weeks scrambling to produce documents the carrier wants. This post is the shortcut.

## The five questions that move your premium the most

In order, highest to lowest impact on the 2026 premium:

1. **"Do you have multi-factor authentication on every account with access to email, customer data, or financial systems?"** — SMS codes no longer qualify on three of the four major carrier forms. Authenticator app or hardware key only.
2. **"Do you have a written Information Security Program (WISP) or written Acceptable Use Policy?"** — Coalition's Q1 sample shows a 14% median premium bump for "no" answers versus a signed, dated document.
3. **"Have you tested a restore from backup in the last 12 months?"** — Not "do you have backups." Tested. Dated. With evidence.
4. **"Do you have a written AI Acceptable Use Policy covering ChatGPT, Copilot, Claude, Gemini, and Notion AI?"** — New in 2026. Nobody who's never had to answer it gets it right on the first try.
5. **"Have you run a tabletop exercise in the last 12 months?"** — Sign-in sheet, scenario document, action list. Not "we talked about it at a meeting."

If you can say "yes, here's the document" to the first four, you're in the lowest-risk tier your size allows. If you can't, the broker has to note "no" on the form, and the rate reflects that.

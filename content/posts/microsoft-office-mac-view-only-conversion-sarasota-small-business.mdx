Microsoft Office 2019 and 2021 for Mac are aging out of the comfortable zone for small offices. The practical risk is simple: a staff member opens Word or Excel expecting to edit, and the app only lets them view the file.

For a one-person home office, that is annoying. For a law office, clinic, accounting firm, real estate desk, or service company trying to send a quote, it can stop the day.

## Short Answer

If your team still depends on Office 2019 or Office 2021 for Mac, inventory those machines now and move editing work to a supported Microsoft 365 Apps install or a planned alternative.

Do not wait until the first staff member reports that a contract, spreadsheet, or intake form is suddenly locked in view-only mode.

## What To Check This Week

- Open Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and Outlook on every Mac and record the exact version.
- Confirm whether the user signs in with a business Microsoft 365 account or a personal account.
- Check whether OneDrive and SharePoint files open in the desktop apps, not only in the browser.
- Verify that shared templates, macros, fonts, and PDF export still work after the upgrade.
- Put at least one test Mac through the new setup before changing every workstation.

If you want a local baseline, start with the [Sarasota IT support checklist](/sarasota-it-support) and compare the Macs against the same identity, backup, and patching standards you use for Windows machines.

## Where Small Offices Get Burned

The license is only one part of the problem. The real trouble usually sits around the edges:

- Old Outlook profiles that have not been cleaned up in years.
- Shared mailboxes that were added by hand instead of through permissions.
- Local-only files sitting in Desktop and Documents with no reliable backup.
- Staff using different Office builds on different machines.
- One Mac that handles payroll, invoices, or client forms and never gets tested until something breaks.

A good upgrade plan protects the workflow, not just the app icon.

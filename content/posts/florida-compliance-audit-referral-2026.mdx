Compliance audits are a specific genre of misery: the firm bills hourly, the scope creeps, the back-and-forth takes weeks, and at the end you're pretty sure you could have gotten the same report for half the price if you'd shopped differently.

After three years of running audit-prep work for Sarasota and Bradenton clients, here is what actually matters when you pick a firm — and where Simple IT SRQ fits in the process.

## The four audits most Florida small businesses deal with

1. **SOC 2 Type II.** Every SaaS customer above a certain size asks for this. Also now on most 2026 cyber-insurance questionnaires. First run is 6-12 months of prep + observation window; renewal is 3-6 months.
2. **HIPAA Risk Assessment + Safeguards Documentation.** Required annually for every practice touching PHI — medical, dental, PT, chiropractic. The written assessment is the first thing OCR asks for in an audit.
3. **PCI DSS.** Required for any business that stores, processes, or transmits cardholder data. Small merchants usually qualify for the SAQ-A self-assessment; bigger operations need an audited Report on Compliance.
4. **FTC Safeguards Rule.** Mandatory since 2023 for "financial institutions" as the FTC defines them — which quietly includes a lot more businesses than people realize (tax preparers, mortgage brokers, wealth managers, auto dealers, even some MSPs).

## The four mistakes that double your audit bill

We've watched several Sarasota clients go through their first audit. The same four mistakes show up every time:

- **Picking on audit type instead of auditor.** Your practice might actually need three of these, not one. If you take credit cards AND store PHI, you're dealing with PCI and HIPAA. Pick a firm that covers both at once — don't hire two separate auditors and pay for the same overlap twice.
- **Starting the engagement without the documents.** Audit firms bill hourly when the paperwork isn't ready. If you have no WISP, no risk assessment, no policy stack, you're paying the auditor $250/hour to wait while you write them. Our [WISP Template](/store/wisp-template), [HIPAA Starter Kit](/store/hipaa-starter-kit), and [Evidence Binder](/store/cyber-insurance-evidence-binder) are designed to front-load that 60-70% of documentation so the billable clock starts later.
- **Hiring a national firm for a small-office engagement.** The big-four consulting arms quote SOC 2 at $60,000+ for a small SaaS because their minimum engagement is sized for large enterprise. Florida regional firms quote the same engagement at $15,000-$25,000 and do better work because they're not rotating a new junior through every week.
- **Not negotiating the scope.** Auditors will happily include every system you casually mention in the scoping call. If your billing system doesn't touch cardholder data, say so explicitly — don't let it get swept into PCI scope. Scope creep is the #1 driver of audit cost overrun.

## Ballpark pricing for Florida small offices

These are ranges we actually see — your specific quote will vary with employee count, scope, and whether you already have documentation in place.

- **SOC 2 Type II (10-50 person SaaS):** $15,000-$40,000 first run; $10,000-$25,000 renewals.
- **HIPAA Risk Assessment (2-20 person practice):** $2,500-$10,000. With our Starter Kit already filled out, closer to the low end.
- **PCI DSS SAQ-A (small merchant):** $500-$2,000 for the self-assessment validation; $8,000+ for an attested ROC.
- **FTC Safeguards (small tax prep / mortgage broker):** $3,000-$8,000 for a first-run.

The cost of NOT doing the audit — when an insurer or customer requires it — is usually the entire contract or policy, so the budget question isn't "can we afford the audit" but "can we afford the engagement to be sloppy and drag."

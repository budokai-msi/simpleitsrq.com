Big market stories are useful only if they make local decisions clearer. The lesson for a Sarasota or Bradenton owner is not about aerospace. It is about discipline: invest first in the systems that keep the business operating.

## The local IT budget test

Before buying another platform, ask whether the basics are already funded:

- Can staff work if the internet, Wi-Fi, or one workstation fails?
- Are backups tested, not just configured?
- Are Microsoft 365, email security, and admin access documented well enough for a renewal questionnaire?
- Does every tool have an owner, a monthly cost, and a reason to exist?

If the answer is no, the next dollar should not go to a trend. It should go to resilience.

## What to cut or keep

Keep tools that remove manual work, protect revenue, or shorten recovery time. Cut tools nobody owns, duplicate apps, and subscriptions that only one person understands. Then use the savings to fix backups, MFA, Wi-Fi, endpoint protection, or documentation.
